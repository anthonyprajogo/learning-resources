Health

High barriers to entry but enormous impact potential. Regulatory complexity creates moats for those who understand the system. Strong NZ-to-Australia pipeline.

Startup
Product / Clinical Tech Lead
Building diagnostic tools, patient platforms, or workflow software. Requires navigating FDA/TGA/Medsafe approval alongside product development. High autonomy, long sales cycles into hospitals.
RegulatoryClinical UXHL7/FHIR

Corporate
Health IT Consultant / EHR Specialist
Implementing or customising enterprise systems (Epic, Oracle Health). Deep integration work between legacy infrastructure and modern platforms. Stable but complex stakeholder environments.
EHR SystemsIntegrationChange Mgmt
P
Public Sector
Digital Health Programme Manager
Driving national health digitisation (e.g. Te Whatu Ora in NZ). Procurement-heavy, politically navigated. Slower but genuinely systemic — decisions affect entire populations.
Te Whatu OraProcurementPolicy

Finance
High velocityWell compensatedMature
+

One of the most developed tech verticals globally. NZ is small but connected to AU. Strong graduate pipeline. Risk and compliance fluency opens serious doors.

S
Startup
Fintech Product Engineer / Growth Lead
Payments, lending, crypto infrastructure, or wealth platforms. Fast iteration, strong equity upside. NZ examples: Sharesies, Pushpay. Often competing directly with banks on UX.
PaymentsOpen BankingGrowth

Corporate
Technology Risk / Digital Transformation
Running core banking modernisation, cloud migration, or AI/ML risk models inside major banks. ANZ, Westpac, BNZ all active in this space. Heavily governed but well-resourced.
Core BankingAI RiskCloud Infra
P
Public Sector
RBNZ / FMA Technology Adviser
Regtech and supervisory technology — helping regulators understand and assess digital financial systems. Niche but influential. Less common entry point but strong for policy-tech intersection.
RegTechSupervisoryPolicy

Inside Fonterra, Zespri, or LIC — driving analytics capability, supply chain optimisation, or farmer-facing digital tools. Meaningful scale, slower culture. Underrated for IS graduates.
Supply ChainAnalyticsFarmer UX

Public Sector
MPI Digital / Biosecurity Systems
Ministry for Primary Industries runs significant data and traceability infrastructure. Biosecurity and environmental compliance tech is a genuine focus. Cross-government data sharing work underway.
MPIBiosecurityTraceability

+

Often underrated by ambitious graduates. Decisions here affect entire populations. Interesting tension between mission alignment and bureaucratic friction. DIA and Stats NZ are genuinely active.

Startup

Building tools that interface with or improve government services — permitting, benefits access, public data APIs. Often mission-driven, grant-funded. Growing ecosystem in UK/US, nascent in NZ.

Corporate

Technology transformation for government agencies — cloud, data, service design. Influence at scale, good for pattern recognition across agencies. Deloitte TST fits squarely here.
Cloud MigrationService DesignDeloitte
P
Public Sector
Digital Government Lead (DIA / Stats NZ)
Running digital service standards, data infrastructure, or open government initiatives from inside. DIA's Service Innovation Lab and Stats NZ's data platform work are legitimately interesting.
DIAStats NZData Infra

Pricing models, grid analytics, customer platform modernisation inside major gentailers. Underrated for tech talent — genuinely complex data problems, real consequences.
Pricing ModelsGrid AnalyticsCX Platforms

Public Sector
MBIE / Transpower Digital Advisor
Energy policy modelling, grid investment planning tools, or market design work at MBIE or Transpower. Directly shapes NZ's long-term energy infrastructure decisions.

Structurally underfunded but genuinely ripe for AI disruption. The most interesting EdTech plays are in the corporate L&D space, not K-12. NZ has a strong international education export angle too.

Startup
EdTech Product Lead
AI tutoring, skills credentialing, corporate L&D platforms. Consumer EdTech has a brutal track record, but enterprise L&D and workforce upskilling plays are more defensible. Global market, capital-light to start.
AI TutoringL&DCredentialing

Corporate
LMS / Digital Learning Architect
Inside large enterprises or tertiary institutions managing learning management systems, content platforms, and workforce capability tools. Underrated technically — complex integrations and analytics challenges.
LMSWorkdayAnalytics

Public Sector
MoE Digital Equity / EdTech Policy
Ministry of Education digital inclusion programmes, school infrastructure, or curriculum technology standards. High mission alignment, limited budget. More interesting if you care about equity than scale.
MoEDigital EquityInfrastructure

Chronically underestimated by tech talent. Enormous complexity, genuine optimisation problems, and real AI applicability. COVID exposed the fragility of global supply chains and capital followed.

Real-time tracking, predictive logistics, freight marketplace, or autonomous warehouse tooling. Huge capital flows post-COVID. Founders with domain knowledge of NZ's import dependency have genuine angles.

SAP/Oracle implementation, demand forecasting, network design inside major retailers or manufacturers. Woolworths NZ, Mainfreight, or Fonterra are NZ examples. Technically deep, well compensated, not flashy.
SAPDemand PlanningNetwork Design

Public Sector
NZTA / Customs Digital Systems
NZ Customs' trade facilitation systems, NZTA freight and infrastructure data, or border tech. Less obvious but genuinely critical infrastructure. National security and trade efficiency overlap here.
NZ CustomsNZTATrade Systems

Construction is one of the least digitised major industries globally — productivity has barely moved in 50 years. That's either a graveyard or a genuine opportunity. NZ's housing crisis gives local urgency.

Startup
PropTech / ConTech Product Lead
Consenting automation, construction project management, real estate transaction platforms, or property data infrastructure. Procore is the global reference point. Auckland's consenting bottleneck is a real problem with startup activity around it.
ConsentingBIMReal Estate Data

Fletcher Building and engineering consultancies like Beca are running genuine digital programmes — BIM adoption, project analytics, supply chain digitisation. Good for operational depth in a traditionally analogue industry.
FletcherBecaBIM Adoption

Building consenting systems, housing data infrastructure, or urban planning tools at MBIE or Auckland Council. Genuinely consequential — consenting process reform is one of the clearest levers on housing supply.
