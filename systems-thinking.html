Systems Thinking:
Seeing the Underlying Structure

AP
Anthony Prajogo April 2026

Most problems aren't isolated events. They're symptoms of systems — interconnected loops of cause and effect that repeat until the underlying structure changes. Systems thinking is the discipline of seeing that structure before it sees you.

The core insight

Linear thinking asks: what caused this? Systems thinking asks: what structure keeps producing this?

One leads to blame. The other leads to leverage.
